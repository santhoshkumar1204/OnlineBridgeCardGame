BID AND BRIDGE

4 PLAYER CARD GAME

GAME RULES

What is Bridge?

Bridge is a classic trick-taking card game played by four players in two teams (called partnerships). Players bid on how many tricks they expect to win and then play to fulfill their contract.

Key Terms:

Partnership – A team of two players.

Trick – A round where each player plays one card.

Bid – A prediction of the number of tricks (1–7) the team expects to win, along with a suit or No Trump.

Pass – Choosing not to make a bid.

Contract – The final winning bid that sets the goal for the round.

Trump / No Trump – The chosen suit that outranks all others, or "No Trump" meaning no suit has priority.

Declarer – The player who wins the bidding and leads the gameplay.

Dummy – The declarer’s partner; their cards are laid face-up after the bidding.

Lead – The first card played in a trick.

How to Play Bridge: Step by Step

Before You Start

Bridge uses a standard 52-card deck.

Each player receives 13 cards.

Players organize their cards by suit.

A new dealer is chosen each round.

Step 1: Bidding

Each player takes turns bidding a number (1–7) and a suit or No Trump.

Each bid must be higher than the previous, or the player can choose to pass.

The highest bid becomes the contract, setting the number of tricks that partnership must win.

Step 2: Gameplay

The declarer leads the first trick.

The dummy reveals their hand face-up.

All players must follow suit if possible.

The highest card wins the trick, unless a trump is played.

Step 3: Winning a Trick

If a trump card is played, the highest trump wins.

If no trumps are played, the highest card of the led suit wins.

A total of 13 tricks are played per round.

At the end, teams count the number of tricks won to determine success.

Strategic Tips for Success

Support your partner’s bids for better coordination.

Pass if your hand totals fewer than 13 points.

Bid 1 No Trump if you have 16–18 balanced points.

With 13+ points, start by bidding your strongest suit.

Observe and adapt based on your opponents' play.
